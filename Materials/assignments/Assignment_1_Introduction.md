# 📚 Assignment 1: Introduction to Spectacular Wonders

**Due Date:** End of Week 1  
**Points:** 20  
**Type:** Individual Written Assignment

---

## 📋 Assignment Overview

In this assignment, you will explore the concept of "spectacular wonders" and identify different types of wonders around the world.

---

## 🎯 Learning Objectives

By completing this assignment, you will:
- Understand what makes something a "spectacular wonder"
- Distinguish between natural and man-made wonders
- Research and document specific wonders
- Develop descriptive writing skills

---

## 📝 Instructions

### Part 1: Definition and Understanding (5 points)

**Write a paragraph (5-7 sentences) answering these questions:**

1. What is a "spectacular wonder"?
2. What characteristics must something have to be considered a wonder?
3. Give 3 examples of things that might be considered wonders

**Example opening:** "A spectacular wonder is..."

---

### Part 3: Research Activity (7 points)

**Choose ONE wonder from the list below and research it:**

**Ancient Wonders:**
- Great Pyramid of Giza
- Hanging Gardens of Babylon
- Temple of Zeus at Olympia
- Colossus of Rhodes
- Mausoleum at Halicarnassus
- Lighthouse of Alexandria
- Statue of Zeus

**Modern Natural Wonders:**
- Mount Everest
- Victoria Falls
- Amazon Rainforest
- Grand Canyon
- Great Barrier Reef
- Serengeti National Park
- Northern Lights

**Write a one-page description including:**
- Name and location
- When it was discovered/built
- Why it's considered a wonder
- Current status (still standing? protected? etc.)
- 3 interesting facts

## 💡 Tips for Success

✨ **Research Tips:**
- Use reliable sources (Wikipedia, National Geographic, educational websites)
- Take notes while researching
- Cite your sources (mention where you found information)

📸 **Screenshot Tips:**
- Zoom to show detail
- Rotate the view for best angle
- Tilt for 3D perspective
- Save in a clear, organized folder

✍️ **Writing Tips:**
- Write in complete sentences
- Use descriptive language
- Check spelling and grammar
- Read your work aloud before submitting
